Why UniClean exists

Every day, people walk past dump sites, polluted waterways, and degraded land with no trusted way to report them or see what happens next. UniClean turns those invisible problems into a visible, public record that anyone can act on.

How it works: post lifecycle

Spotted

Someone photographs a polluted site, pins GPS coordinates, adds a short description, and posts it publicly.

Claimed

Any individual, group, business, NGO, or government body can publicly claim the cleanup and become accountable.

In progress

Optional updates show the cleanup happening, keeping communities engaged and informed.

Resolved

After-photos and a short report are posted, and the status flips to Resolved as a permanent public record.

Unclaimed

Reports that no one claims within a set time are escalated and highlighted on the map as pressure points.

Who UniClean is for

Community members

Youth, students, residents, and traders who see pollution and want to report it and track what happens.

Responders

Individuals, youth groups, NGOs, businesses, and government bodies ready to claim and resolve cleanups.

Partners & funders

UN agencies, climate funds, researchers, and media who need reliable field data and visible impact stories.
